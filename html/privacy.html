In brief, you should know:

  • You are anonymous for this service, unless you are 1) identified as an administrator or 2) enrolled in any of the courses that this service manages.
  • University of Turku Single Sign-On is trusted explicitly. Your credentials are not stored in this system.
  • Web server access logs are anonymous. The automatically collected usage statistics (access and error logs) contain the usual information, such as IP, browser type, browser version, etc., but are otherwise completely anonymous and (within the capabilities of this service) cannot be connected to any data that identifies you as a person.
  • Enrolled students have their UID, name, email and student ID stored. These are the minimum required details to enable course grading to be delivered to university systems.

What data do we collect?

Following data is imported to this service:

Students registered to a course managed by this system:

  • UTU Single Sign-On User ID
  • Name
  • Student ID
  • Email Address

This website collects the following data:

From students:

  • GitHub account (used to submit course execises)
  • Submission details (date and time, score, feedback from evaluating assistant)

From everyone else:

  • Nothing

How do we collect your data?

Personal data (listed above) is not collected by this system, but imported from UTU Peppi system, with the exception of GitHub account. For anonymous and student access, this website does not collect nor store any other identifiable personal data. System will collect score and grading information from other systems and add them to user data.

Users identified as administrators or course assistants will agree to have his or her UTU Single Sign-On (henceforth referred to as SSO) ID stored in the website's list of administrators or course assistants permanently and to be used in security audit logging.

How will we use your data?

Student submissions are used to check that required assignments are passed and to calculate course grade when the course is completed. Email is used to send automated messages notifying of deadlines, provided feedback for submissions, and to warn of any problems found in submissions.

Administrators and course assistants have their activities logged for security reasons, in case students note irregularities in their data and/or submissions.

How do we store your data?

This website stores all data into a locally-only accessible RDBMS, in a virtual server instance, securely hosted and maintained by UTU IT-Services.

This website will keep student's personal data for as long as standing orders require to keep them (for audits or examining data for contested results). Student data is removed annually when no longer subject to univeristy archival requirements.

Marketing

This website neither engages in any type of marketing nor share any of the data with external systems or parties.

What are your data protection rights?

University of Turku would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

The right to access – You have the right to request us for copies of your personal data. We may charge you a small fee for this service. Fees are subject to change without notification, on hourly basis.

The right to rectification – You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate. You also have the right to request us to complete the information you believe is incomplete.

The right to erasure – You have the right to request that this service erase your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to restrict processing – You have the right to request that this website restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to object to processing – You have the right to object to this service processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to data portability – You have the right to request us to transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.

If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact the lecturer.

What are cookies

Cookies are text files placed on your computer to collect standard Internet log information and visitor behavior information. When you visit our websites, we may collect information from you automatically through cookies or similar technology.

For further information, visit allaboutcookies.org.

How to manage cookies

You can set your browser not to accept cookies, and the above website tells you how to remove cookies from your browser. However, this will render the features of this website non-functional and may prevent successful completion of the course.
